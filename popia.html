3. Definitions (POPIA Section 1)

Personal Information: Any info relating to an identifiable living natural person or juristic person (where applicable). Includes email, phone 076 948 9154, device ID, IP, M365 login metadata.
Special Personal Information (Sec 26-33): Religious beliefs, race, health, biometric data, criminal behaviour. We do NOT intentionally collect this, except where a client stores it in M365 we protect.
Data Subject: Person to whom personal info relates – e.g., your staff whose laptop we support.
Responsible Party: Entity deciding why and how personal info is processed. NextGrid is Responsible Party for our CRM, billing, site.
Operator: Processes personal info for Responsible Party under contract. NextGrid is Operator when we manage your tenant – we act only on your written instructions.
Processing: Any operation including collection, storage, backup, transmission, restriction, deletion. Includes our RMM pulling device health.
Information Officer: Sec 55-appointed person ensuring POPIA compliance, handling DSARs, liaison with Regulator.
BEC / Ransomware: Business Email Compromise – invoice email fraud common in SA SMEs. Ransomware – malware encrypting data during vulnerable periods (e.g., load shedding reboots).

8. Security Measures (Section 19) – Technical & Organisational

Appropriate, reasonable measures for SA SME threat reality: ransomware, BEC invoice fraud, stolen laptops, load shedding corruption, insider misuse.

Technical Measures
  • Identity: MFA number matching enforced, Conditional Access: block outside SA, block legacy auth (Basic, POP, IMAP), risky sign-in step-up, 14-day sign-in review.
  • Email Security: Defender Safe Links / Safe Attachments, anti-spoof SPF/DKIM/DMARC p=quarantine, DLP policies for SA ID (13-digit), bank account (branch code), credit card.
  • Endpoint & Network: EDR with rollback (ransomware undo), Firewall IPS, WPA3 + separate guest VLAN, BitLocker AES-256 + PIN, Autopilot wipe for lost devices.
  • Data Protection: M365 sensitivity labels (Confidential encrypt), transport encryption TLS 1.2+, at-rest encryption, immutable backups offsite in SA DC (Teraco), weekly restore test documented.
  • Resilience – Load Shedding: UPS for all on-prem gear, graceful shutdown scripts, backup integrity check after power event, cloud-first M365 to avoid local NAS corruption.
  • Logging: M365 Unified Audit Logs kept 1yr+, RMM logs 3yrs, SIEM alerts 24/7, tamper-proofing via separate log account.
Organisational Measures
  • Least Privilege: No shared admin accounts, daily user vs Global Admin break-glass, access review quarterly, offboarding within 2h – M365 sessions revoked, device wipe.
  • Training: Monthly 5-min security tips for our team + client staff, BEC double-check via phone 076 948 9154 for any bank change, phishing simulation via Defender Attack Simulator.
  • POPIA by Design: DPIA for any new processing (especially new RMM tool), privacy default minimal, data flow map updated, vendor DPA checklist before onboarding.
  • Physical: Laptops asset-tagged, BIOS password, screen lock 5min, clean desk, on-site visits Pretoria/Centurion ID-verified, client data never on USB without encrypted container.
  • Contracts: Client MSA includes POPIA Sec 20 operator clauses, breach 24h SLA, annual R950 Check verifies technical proof (Secure Score >70, DLP enabled, retention labels).
  • Insurance & Audit: Cyber liability cover, annual self-assessment + external spot check, backup restore drill recorded for Regulator proof.
Proof you can show clients: We give you audit export: MFA report, Conditional Access policies, DLP hits (no content), backup test cert, Secure Score. Helps you answer your own POPIA questionnaire.

9. Data Subject Rights & How to Submit a Request (Sec 23-25, Form 2)

You have the right to: be notified, access, correction, deletion, objection to processing, objection to direct marketing, not be subject to solely automated decision-making, submit complaint to Regulator.

  1. Choose channel: Email info@nextgridtechnologies.co.za with subject POPIA REQUEST – [Access/Correction/Deletion/Objection] – Your Name OR call 076 948 9154 for urgent guidance, then follow up in writing.
  2. Provide these fields (Form 2): Full name + surname, SA ID or passport (redact where possible – we need to verify), company, relationship to us (client employee / vendor / site visitor), best contact email + phone, specific request description (e.g., “all ticket logs for johan@client.co.za from Jan 2025”), preferred format (PDF/email), proof of ID (certified copy) and if acting for someone, power of attorney.

  4. We process & respond: Within 30 calendar days per POPIA (or 35 days if complex with extension notice). Deletion respects legal retention (e.g., tax 5yrs). Access is free unless manifestly excessive (fee per PAIA tariffs, quoted beforehand).
  5. Escalate if needed: If not satisfied, use complaints process below.

11. Cross-Border Transfers & Safeguards (Section 72)

SA SME reality: Microsoft 365 does not guarantee 100% SA-only processing. Email metadata and threat intel may be processed in EU/US. We minimise this.

  • Preference SA: Backups stored in Johannesburg/Cape Town DC with AES-256 and keys held by us. No backup leaves SA without written consent.
  • Lawful grounds for transfer: (a) Necessary for contract performance (your M365 license), (b) Consent via MSA, (c) Transfers to country with adequate protection or under binding agreement.
  • Safeguards: Microsoft DPA + Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), UK Addendum where applicable, encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest, data residency settings (South Africa tenant region = EU data boundary, not SA but closest). For RMM US processing, DPA with SCCs + least data – only device health, not mailbox content.
What we don’t do: We never transfer ID numbers, bank details, or special personal info outside SA for our own purposes. If transfer needed for support (e.g., Microsoft support case), we anonymize or get your prior consent and log it.
